# Northwind Materials Inc. — FY2025 Form 10-K (excerpt)

## Item 1A. Risk Factors — Customer Concentration

For the fiscal year ended December 31, 2025, sales to our single largest
customer, Pallas Automotive Group, represented approximately **62% of total net
revenue** (2024: 58%; 2023: 51%). Our second-largest customer accounted for a
further 11%. The concentration of revenue among a small number of customers has
increased over the past three years. The loss of, or a material reduction in
orders from, Pallas Automotive Group would have a material adverse effect on our
business, financial condition, and results of operations. Our master supply
agreement with Pallas Automotive Group expires in December 2026 and contains no
minimum-volume commitment.

## Item 9. Changes in and Disagreements with Accountants

On February 3, 2025, the Audit Committee of the Board of Directors dismissed
Barrow & Finch LLP as the Company's independent registered public accounting
firm and engaged Crestline Audit LLP effective the same date. The report of
Barrow & Finch LLP on the Company's financial statements for the two most recent
fiscal years contained no adverse opinion or disclaimer of opinion. There were
no disagreements with Barrow & Finch LLP on any matter of accounting principles
or practices.

## Note 18. Going Concern

The Company has evaluated conditions and events and concluded that substantial
doubt about its ability to continue as a going concern does **not** exist.
